About Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci was an Italian painter, inventor, and scientist, often described as the ultimate "Renaissance man." Born in Vinci, Italy, he created masterpieces such as the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. Beyond art, he designed inventions like flying machines, early tanks, and anatomical sketches far ahead of his time. His curiosity and genius left a lasting impact on both art and science.

Important Events

  1. 1452 - Born in Vinci, Italy.
  2. 1469 - Began apprenticeship with Andrea del Verrocchio in Florence.
  3. 1482 - Moved to Milan to work for Duke Ludovico Sforza.
  4. 1503 - Started painting the "Mona Lisa" in Florence.
  5. 1516 - Moved to France under the patronage of King Francis I.
